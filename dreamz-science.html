The Neuroscience

Grounded in how the brain actually sleeps.

Sleep is an active, structured process. The brain moves through distinct states, each defined by its own patterns of activity. These patterns are not random. They reflect how the brain restores the body, consolidates memory, and regulates emotion.

When these patterns become unstable or delayed, sleep quality suffers. You may take longer to fall asleep, wake more often, or feel less restored.

Research shows the brain is responsive to subtle external signals, especially when they align with its current state. By supporting natural rhythms at the right moments, the brain can transition more smoothly into and through sleep.
